Projects

Dungeon Duelists

The project I worked on during my graduation and am still working on is Dungeon Duelists. It's an online-multiplayer 8-player Battle Royale that is heavily inspired by roguelikes. It features a procedurally generated dungeon that I personally created. Dungeon Duelists is made in Unity 6.0 using Netcode for GameObjects. A free demo will be uploaded on Steam during Steam Next Fest on the 13th of October and will be released shortly after.

3D Graphics Engine

I spent a 5 weeks working on my own primitive 3D graphics engine. It came as a bare bones C++ library that used Direct3D 12 for its render API and fast_obj to read OBJ files.

Sorting machine

I spent 10 weeks working on a sorting robot in a small team. The goal was to have the robot arm visualize sorting algorithms to potential new students during an open house. It used the PhantomX AX-12 mechanical arm along with an Arduino that sent movement commands to the arm. A mounted camera combined with a Raspberry Pi ran an computer vision AI model communicated with the Arduino.

DOOM-like shooter

I spent 10 weeks working on a fast-paced first-person shooter that was heavily inspired by the DOOM series. Me and a group of peers were given an already existing project and given a lot of freedom to improve it in whatever way we saw fit. We changed the design of the game around and added new content and after our work was done another group took over the project. The project was an Unreal Engine project and during this time I worked a lot on improving my Unreal Engine skills. I had previous experience with Unreal before I did game development but still gained a lot more in-depth experience.
